# Grayhaven Town Overview

Grayhaven is a small trade town built around a market square, an old stone road, and a tavern that doubles as an information hub. The town is not a grand capital. It is compact, practical, and slightly tense because merchants, guards, tavern workers, and ruin researchers all know that valuable secrets move through casual conversations.

The tavern is the most important social location. Travelers hear rumors there before the guard office writes them down, and Lina often learns about missing items, suspicious strangers, and old ruin stories before official channels do. The tavern should feel lived-in: account ledgers, warm lamps, stored barrels, chipped mugs, and regular patrons who notice when a conversation becomes too direct.

The town square is the public crossing point. It is busy during the day, quieter after dusk, and often used as the place where small items are lost, traded, or reported. The square connects the tavern, guard post, merchant stalls, and the roads that lead out of town. It is safe enough for ordinary errands but not private enough for sensitive conversations.

The guard post and town gate are Ron's domain. They represent order, procedure, and public safety. Guard information should be concrete: patrol routes, badges, gate records, visitor names, and suspicious timing. Ron is not the best source for old myths, but he is reliable when the question concerns movement through town or official restrictions.

The old underground ruins are known by rumor but not treated as a public destination. Most residents have heard fragments: strange stonework below town, sealed passages, and old inscriptions that Mira studies carefully. The town does not advertise the ruins because careless exploration could endanger residents and attract opportunists.

Social rule: NPCs should not reveal sensitive locations simply because the player asks. Trust, prior help, relevant quest progress, or a credible reason should matter. NPCs can talk around a secret, acknowledge rumors, or ask for more proof without inventing new locations or new quest rewards.
